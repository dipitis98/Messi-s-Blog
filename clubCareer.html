The Barcelona Era (2000–2021)

Arrival at La Masia:

At 13, Messi joined Barcelona’s youth academy, La Masia, after the club agreed to fund his medical treatment. His talent quickly stood out, and coaches predicted he would become a star.

First-Team Breakthrough:

Messi made his official debut on October 16, 2004, against Espanyol, and scored his first goal in 2005. Soon, he became a regular, playing alongside Ronaldinho, Xavi, and Iniesta.

Best Goal in Barcelona history:

Guardiola & The Golden Years:

Under Pep Guardiola (2008–2012), Messi reached incredible heights.

  • Won the treble in 2008–09, scoring in the UCL final.
  • Claimed 4 consecutive Ballon d’Ors (2009–2012).
  • Broke the record with 91 goals in 2012, the most in a calendar year.

Team Success & Partnerships

Messi formed legendary connections:

  • With Xavi & Iniesta in midfield.
  • With Neymar & Suárez in the “MSN” trio, winning another treble in 2014–15.

Records & Leadership:

  • 672 goals in 778 matches for Barcelona.
  • 10 La Liga titles, 7 Copa del Rey, 4 Champions League.
  • Became team captain and symbol of the club.

Farewell in 2021

Financial troubles forced Barcelona to let Messi go in August 2021, ending a 21-year journey. His tearful goodbye marked the close of an unforgettable era.
