Argentina Career & World Cup Glory

Early Days with Argentina

Messi debuted for the Argentine national team in 2005 at age 18. Despite his talent, early tournaments brought heartbreak:

  • 2006 World Cup: Young Messi made an impression but Argentina was eliminated in the quarter-finals.
  • 2014 World Cup: Reached the final but lost 1–0 to Germany in extra time.

Copa América Triumph:

After several near-misses, Messi finally lifted an international trophy in 2021

  • Copa América 2021: Argentina defeated Brazil 1–0 in the final.
  • Messi won the Golden Ball as the tournament’s best player, solidifying his international legacy.

2022 FIFA World Cup:

The pinnacle of Messi’s career came in Qatar 2022:

  • Led Argentina to victory, defeating France in the final (penalty shootout after a 3–3 draw).
  • Scored 7 goals in the tournament, earning the Golden Ball as the best player.
  • Finally fulfilled his lifelong dream of winning the World Cup, cementing his status as one of the greatest of all time.

Second Consecutive Copa América Title:

Messi led Argentina to their second consecutive Copa América victory, defeating Colombia 1–0 in the final held at Hard Rock Stadium in Miami. This win marked Argentina's record-breaking 16th Copa América title and Messi's third major international tournament win as captain.
